Structure of the block

  • In this block there are 2 main tasks. You need to:
    • forecast the First Mover's choice, and
    • forecast the Second Mover's choice.
  • You will participate in 15 Forecast problems.
  • Each problem is equally likely to be selected for payment.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each problem seriously.

Structure of task 1

  • There are 5 payment structures along with one forecast in each payment structure. You need to:
    • forecast First Mover's decision.
  • You would see the similar interface for each payment structure:

Second Mover choice

  • You are asked to forecast the percentage interval of First Mover's choice:
    • What do you think is the percent chance that First Mover would choose A?
      • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%

Structure of the task 2

  • There are 5 payment structures along with two forecasts in each payment structure. You need to:
    • learn the choice of the First Mover, then
    • forecast the Second Mover's decision
  • You would see the similar interface for each payment structure:

First Mover payment Second Mover payment
First Mover choice           A           Second Mover choice       A 350 tokens 400 tokens
      B 200 tokens 500 tokens
          B           Second Mover choice       A 600 tokens 200 tokens
      B 30 tokens 25 tokens

  • You are asked to forecast the percentage interval of the Second Mover's choice:
    • What do you think is the percent chance that the Second Mover would choose A given the First Mover chose A?
      • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%
    • What do you think is the percent chance that the Second Mover would choose A given the First Mover chose B?
      • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%
  • Note that every forecast is important to determine your payment as until the end of the experiment you do not know what other participant has chosen.

Payments

  • One out of 15 forecasts will be chosen at random.
  • One participant in this room (excluding you) will be picked at random and this participant's decision in block 2 will be retrieved.
  • If the choice picked by the selected participant is A, the forecast error = (1-your choice)².
  • If the choice picked by the selected participant is B, the forecast error = (your choice)².
  • A uniform random number will be drawn form the interval from 0 to 1;
  • You will receive £5 if the forecast error is below the random number and will not receive any additional payment otherwise;


  • NOTE   Since your forecasts are made when you do not know what other participants have chosen. The best thing you can do to gain the highest payment in this block is to simply state what you think.
