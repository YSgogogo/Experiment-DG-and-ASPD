Structure of the Block

  • In this block there are 2 main tasks. You need to:
    • forecast First Mover's choice, and
    • forecast Second Mover's choice.
  • You will participate in 15 Forecast problems.
  • Each problem is equally likely to be selected for payment.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each problem seriously.

  • You will see 5 different payment structures. For each you will need to:

  • Assume First Mover has chosen A, what do you think is the percent chance that Second Mover would choose A?
    • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%
  • Assume First Mover has chosen B, what do you think is the percent chance that Second Mover would choose A?
    • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%

  • You are asked to make 10 forecasts in Task 2.
  • Note that every forecast is important to determine your payment because until the end of the experiment you do not know what other participant has chosen.

Payments

The payments for Block 3 are determined as follows.
  • One out of 15 forecasts will be chosen at random.
  • One participant in this room (excluding you) will be picked at random and this participant's decision in Block 2 will be retrieved.
  • If the decision picked by the selected participant is A, the forecast error = (1-your answer) ².
  • If the decision picked by the selected participant is B, the forecast error = (your answer) ².
  • A uniform random number will be drawn form the interval from 0 to 1;
  • You will receive £5 if the forecast error is below the random number and will not receive any additional payment otherwise;


  • NOTE:   Since your forecasts are made when you do not know what other participants have chosen. The best thing you can do to gain the highest payment in this block is to simply state what you think.
