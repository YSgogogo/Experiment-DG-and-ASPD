BLOCK 3: Instructions


Structure of the block

  • In this block there are 2 main tasks.
    • Guessing the First Mover's choice, and
    • Guessing the Second Mover's choice.
  • You will participate in 12 guessing problems.
  • Each problem is equally likely to be selected for payment.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each problem seriously.

Structure of the task 1

  • There are 4 guessing problems in task 1. You need to:
    • guess the First Mover's decision.
  • you would see the following interface:

Second Mover choice

First Mover payment Second Mover payment First Mover payment Second Mover payment
First Mover choice A 35 tokens 40 tokens 20 tokens 50 tokens
B 60 tokens 20 tokens 30 tokens 25 tokens

  • You are asked to guess the percentage interval of the First Mover's choice:
    • What do you think is the percent chance that the First Mover would choose A?
    • You need to choose one out on following 7 choices
      • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%
  • The above table only shows one payment structure that you need to make one guess
  • You are asked to make four guesses with four different payment structures.

Structure of the task 2

  • There are 8 guessing problems in task 2. You need to:
    • learn the choice of the First Mover, then
    • guess the Second Mover's decision
  • you would see the following interface:

First Mover payment Second Mover payment
First Mover choice           A           Second Mover choice       A 35 tokens 40 tokens
      B 20 tokens 50 tokens
          B           Second Mover choice       A 60 tokens 20 tokens
      B 30 tokens 25 tokens

  • You are asked to guess the percentage interval of the Second Mover's choice:
    • What do you think is the percent chance that the Second Mover would choose A given the First Mover chose A?
    • You need to choose one out on following 7 choices
      • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%
    • What do you think is the percent chance that the Second Mover would choose A given the First Mover chose A?
    • You need to choose one out on following 7 choices
      • 0%, 1%-20%, 21%-40%, 41%-60%, 61%-80%, 81%-99%, 100%
  • The above table only shows one payment structure that you need to make two conditional guesses
  • You are asked to make eight conditional guesses with four different payment structures.
  • Note that every guess is important to determine the outcome as until the end of the experiment you do not know what they exactly choose.

How your additional payment in this block is calculated:

  • You will be able to earn up to £5 in addition to your earnings in other blocks.
  • You will receive the additional payment if you are able to outperform the random machine in guessing the choice of others.
  • After you submit all guessing problems, your earnings will be calculated by the following procedure:
    • One out of 12 guessing questions will be chosen at random;
    • One participant (anonymous) in this room (excluding you) will be picked at random and her/his decision will be retrieved;
    • Your guessing error is computed as (1-your choice)² if the choice picked by the selected participant is A and (your choice)² if the decision picked by the selected participant is B;
    • A uniform random number will be drawn form the interval from 0 to 1;
    • You will receive £5 (in addition to money you already earned) if your prediction error is below the random number and will not receive any additional payment otherwise;
    • For example:
      • The guessing question 4 is randomly chosen, and you have selected 1%-20%. If,
        • The First Mover chose A in the corresponding payment structure, and the random number that is generated by computer is 0.7
          • So your guessing error is (1-your choice)²: (80% to 99%)²= 0.64 to 0.9801, because 0.7 > 0.64. So you get £5
        • The First Mover chose A in the corresponding payment structure, and the random number that is generated by computer is 0.5
          • So your guessing error is (1-your choice)²: (80% to 99%)²= 0.64 to 0.9801, because 0.5 < 0.64. So you get £0
        • The First Mover chose B in the corresponding payment structure, and the random number that is generated by computer is 0.3
          • So your guessing error is (your choice)²: (1% to 20%)²= 0.0001 to 0.04, because 0.3 > 0.0001. So you get £5
        • The First Mover chose B in the corresponding payment structure, and the random number that is generated by computer is 0
          • So your guessing error is (your choice)²: (1% to 20%)²= 0.0001 to 0.04, because 0 < 0.0001. So you get £0
    • You will only see outcomes in the end of the experiment.
  • Keep in mind, if your guess is more close to others' real choice, the higher probability that you will earn £5.
  • Read this page carefully, we will test your comprehension in the next page.
