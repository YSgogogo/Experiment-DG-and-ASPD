Structure of the block

  • In this block there are 2 active players: First Mover and Second Mover.
  • In this block you will face 18 rounds:
    • 9 assuming the role of First Mover, and,
    • 9 assuming the role of Second Mover.
  • Each round you will face one decision problem if you are First Mover and two decision problems if you are Second Mover
  • Each round is independent of the other rounds.
  • Each decision problem in the whole block is equally likely to be selected for your payment.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each round and each decision question seriously.

Structure of the round

  • There are two players in each game in this block.
  • First Mover:
    • moves first
    • chooses A or B
  • Second Mover:
    • observes the choice of First Mover, then
    • decides whether to choose A or B
  • The table below provides an example of the payoff structure depending on the decisions of First Mover and Second Mover:

Second Mover choice

First Mover choice A 350 tokens 400 tokens 150 tokens 500 tokens

  • That is, if First Mover chooses:
    • A, then if Second Mover chooses:
      • A, then First Mover would receive 350 tokens and Second Mover would receive 400 tokens;
      • B, then First Mover would receive 200 tokens and Second Mover would receive 500 tokens.
    • B, then if Second Mover chooses:
      • A, then First Mover would receive 600 tokens and Second Mover would receive 200 tokens;
      • B, then First Mover would receive 300 tokens and Second Mover would receive 250 tokens.

  • As First Mover you need to take decision of A or B
  • You will be presented with the following interface:

Decision of Second Mover

  • As Second Mover you need to take decision of A or B twice
    • Choose A or B given First Mover has chosen A
    • Choose A or B given First Mover has chosen B
  • You will be presented with the following interface:

  • That is, you need to take two decisions:
    • Choose A or B if First Mover chose A (the Yellow row)
    • Choose A or B if First Mover chose B (the Gray row)
  • Note that both decisions are important to determine the outcome because until the end of the experiment you do not know what First Mover will choose.

Payments

The payments for Block 2 are determined as follows.
  • One of the rounds will be randomly selected for payment.
    • Your role in this round is recorded.
    • You are randomly matched with another player in the room.
    • Another player take the opposite role (i.e. if you are First Mover in this round, another player will be Second Mover; if you are Second Mover, another player is First Mover.).
  • The decision you and another player made will be implemented (in the corresponding roles) and thus the payoff will be determined.
  • You will only see outcomes at the end of the experiment.
  • Your payment: 100 tokens = £1
