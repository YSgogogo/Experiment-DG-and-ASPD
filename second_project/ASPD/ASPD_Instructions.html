Structure of the block

  • In this block there are 2 active players: First Mover and Second Mover.
  • You will participate in 18 decision problems.
  • Each decision problem is equally likely to be selected for payment.
  • Each round is independent of other rounds.
  • Thus, it is important for you to take each round seriously.
  • You will face 9 rounds, each round contains 2 sub-rounds:
    • assuming role of the First Mover, and
    • assuming role of the Second Mover.

Structure of the round

  • There are two players in each game in this block.
  • First Mover:
    • moves first
    • chooses A or B
  • Second Mover:
    • observes the choice of First Mover, then
    • decides whether to choose A or B
  • The table below provides an example of the payoff structure depending on the decisions of First Mover and Second Mover:

Second Mover choice
      A       B
First Mover payment Second Mover payment First Mover payment Second Mover payment
First Mover choice A 350 tokens 400 tokens 200 tokens 500 tokens
B 600 tokens 200 tokens 300 tokens 250 tokens

  • That is, if First Mover chooses:
    • A, then if Second Mover chooses:
      • A, then First Mover would receive 350 tokens and Second Mover would receive 400 tokens
      • B, then First Mover would receive 200 tokens and Second Mover would receive 500 tokens
    • B, then if Second Mover chooses:
      • A, then First Mover would receive 600 tokens and Second Mover would receive 200 tokens
      • B, then First Mover would receive 300 tokens and Second Mover would receive 250 tokens

Decision of the First Mover

  • As the First Mover player you would see the following interface:

    • Second Mover choice
    A B

  • Once you take the decision you can proceed to the next decision problem.

    • Decision of the Second Mover

  • As the Second Mover you need to take decision of A or B
  • Given that the decision can be conditioned on the action of the First Mover, you would be presented with the following interface:


    • First Mover payment Your payment
    First Mover choice           A           Your choice
    (Second Mover)     		A 350 tokens 400 tokens
    B 200 tokens 500 tokens
              B           Your choice
    (Second Mover)     		A 600 tokens 200 tokens
    B 300 tokens 250 tokens

    • That is, you need to take two decisions:
      • Choose A or B if First Mover chose A (the yellow row)
      • Choose A or B if First Mover chose B (the gray row)
    • Note that both decisions are important to determine the outcome as until the end of the experiment you do not know what the First Mover would choose.

    Payments

    • The payments for each round are determined as follows.
    • You are randomly matched with another player in the room.
    • You are randomly assigned a role (First Mover or Second Mover).
    • The decision you and another player made would be implemented (in the corresponding roles) and thus the payoff would be determined.
    • One of the rounds will be selected for payment.
    • You will only see outcomes in the end of the experiment.
